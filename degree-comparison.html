// Florida Poly MSME — 4+1 Program Details

Florida Poly offers a 4+1 accelerated master's pathway — you can begin graduate coursework as a senior undergrad and finish in just one additional year. The program has two tracks:

COURSE-ONLY TRACK · 30 CREDITS
Faster completion (3 semesters). Good for industry-bound students. No thesis required. Includes Engineering Management electives.
THESIS TRACK · 30 CREDITS
Extends to 4–5 semesters. Best for R&D, defense, or PhD path. Includes original research. Strongest differentiator on resume.

// Bottom line — for Neil specifically

The 4+1 MS is the high-value move — if R&D or robotics is the goal.

Your portfolio is already strong — autonomous vehicles, Stirling engines, four-bar mechanisms, and documented FEA work puts you above average for a 3rd-year student. A BS alone is absolutely a viable path — especially to get into mechatronics, design, or manufacturing roles at companies in Florida's growing tech and defense corridors.


That said, Florida Poly's 4+1 program changes the math. For just one extra year (with assistantship possibilities offsetting cost), you pick up an ~$15k annual salary advantage at career start, unlock defense and aerospace employers (Lockheed, Raytheon, L3 all pay MS grads $10–30k more), and build research credibility that your robotics/autonomous systems work is perfectly positioned to leverage. The EML 5210 Modern Controls and EML 5040 Numerical Simulation courses directly extend your AV capstone work.


Recommended path: Secure an internship in your 3rd year (you're actively seeking one — great), use that experience to decide between industry-first or 4+1. If you land a strong internship with a defense or robotics firm and they signal interest in hiring, BS → industry → MS part-time is equally valid. If no clear hire signal, the 4+1 is low-risk and high-return.
